Figurative Art

My daughters are my primary inspiration. I'm drawn to the form, movement, and change of the female body. I grew up in Zimbabwe with an ethnomusicologist father—nights of marimbas, mbiras, drums, and dust—always both insider and outsider. That rhythm followed me to Sweden; I danced as a child and as a mother, and it lives in my daughters. Watching how they move, hold themselves, and navigate belonging shapes my work. Like me, they've straddled cultures—appearing to fit while feeling other—learning to carry difference and its loneliness. As they've grown, they've claimed that difference with pride. Their embodied awareness continues to guide my art.

Dualistic Art

Living between cultures shaped a core idea in my work. I juxtapose real and imagined images projecting poems and my own paintings onto my daughters’ bodies—so two fields become one. These “tattoos” try to speak a new story and empower them. Each piece illuminates the shared narratives of mothers and daughters and invites a wider conversation about what girls carry in today’s world. I’m a painter and a storyteller. Every painting is paired with a written tale about who my daughters are, who they’re becoming, and the quiet magic they hold. These modern fairytales confront the everyday challenges young women face—searching for courage, voice, beauty, imagination. As a mother-artist, I’m committed to preserving storytelling as a grounding thread amid the noise. My paintings are repositories of growth, memory, identity, and hope—a way of saying: We were here. We lived. We loved. This is what it meant.

Perimenopausal Art

My work explores selfhood through large acrylic self-portraits of the female body. As I age, I’m re-creating who I am as a woman and mother. Lilith reflects my perimenopausal era—a figure from legend who was cast out for insisting on equality. I relate to her exile: my body has ended fertility without consent, reshaping my sense of womanhood. Yet this “banishment” is also a rite of passage into a second act. These paintings are a practice of self-expression and agency. I challenge my own ideas of beauty and aging, reclaiming my image and reinventing myself. By revealing myself—physically and emotionally—I invite you to witness an unapologetic acceptance of this stage of life. The vibrant nudes declare empowerment and reject ageist limits, affirming the vitality and richness that exist beyond youth.
